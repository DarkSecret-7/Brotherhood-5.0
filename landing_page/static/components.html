5. Auxiliaries & Clienteles
Absorbing Existing Structures

The Brotherhood absorbs participation through two gradual interfaces: Auxiliaries (existing organizations that adopt our standards) and Clienteles (organizations that hire Brotherhood workers). This strategy prevents the all-or-nothing dynamic of most alternative models.

How It Connects: The SAEP System

These components don't operate in isolation. Together, they form the Standardised Academic-Economic Protocol (SAEP)‚Äîa self-reinforcing loop that turns labor power into political power.

How it works: The system replaces the broken "learn then earn" model with a continuous cycle. You learn skills (mapped by the Skill Graph) which you immediately apply in Federations. Your peers verify your capability through Work-Based Assessment, creating a portable record of trust. This verified data allows Unified Work Allocation to automatically match you with new opportunities, removing the need for traditional job markets.

Why it works: By standardizing how we define and verify skills, we create a liquid labor market owned by workers, not corporations. This coordination prevents exploitation: when we control the data of who can do what, we control the distribution of work itself.
