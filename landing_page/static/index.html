Coordination Infrastructure

Building worker power through coordination protocols

The Brotherhood is shared infrastructure for people who create real value but rarely get to shape the systems they work in. It turns scattered skills and informal crews into a coordinated network that can learn, work, and negotiate together.

The Crisis We Face

‚ö†

Workers create value, but do not control how that value is organized or rewarded. Skills are everywhere, yet hiring feels random and disconnected from actual capability.

People who could be collaborators are pushed into competing for the same narrow slots. The Brotherhood treats this as a coordination failure, not a personal failing.

The fragmentation is systematic: Knowledge is siloed in institutions, skills are gatekept by credentials, and workers are isolated from each other. This prevents us from seeing our collective power and organizing effectively.

We're building infrastructure to reconnect what has been deliberately separated‚Äî skills, work, and the people who perform them.

Core Components

‚öôÔ∏è

The Brotherhood is built on five foundational pillars that work together to coordinate labor without centralized control.

Our protocol combines a Skill Graph for mapping human capabilities, Work-Based Assessment for verifying skills through actual projects, Federations for autonomous worker organization, Unified Work Allocation for coordinating labor without central planning, and Auxiliaries & Clienteles for gradually absorbing existing structures.

Together, these components form the Standardised Academic-Economic Protocol (SAEP)‚Äîa self-reinforcing loop that turns labor power into political power by creating a liquid labor market owned by workers, not corporations.
