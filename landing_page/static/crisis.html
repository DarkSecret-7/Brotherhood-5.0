The Crisis of Modern Work

⚠

Workers create value, but do not control how that value is organized or rewarded. Skills are everywhere, yet hiring feels random.

A Broken Market for Talent

The current labor market is fundamentally inefficient. It relies on crude signals like university degrees and brand-name employers to determine worth. This leaves millions of talented individuals invisible because they lack the "correct" credentials, even if they possess the necessary skills.

This isn't just unfair; it's a massive waste of human potential. When we gatekeep opportunity behind expensive and exclusive institutions, we stifle innovation and slow down progress.

Isolation and Exploitation

Without collective bargaining power or a shared infrastructure, workers are isolated. This isolation makes them vulnerable to exploitation. Platforms and corporations can dictate terms, lower wages, and demand more for less because individual workers have little leverage to negotiate.

The "gig economy" promised freedom but often delivers precarity. Workers are pitted against each other in a race to the bottom, competing on price rather than quality or capability.

The Coordination Failure

People who could be collaborators are pushed into competing for the same narrow slots. The Brotherhood treats this as a coordination failure, not a personal failing. Instead of asking individual workers to "stand out" in a broken market, we need shared tools so crews of workers can see each other, trust each other, and move together.
